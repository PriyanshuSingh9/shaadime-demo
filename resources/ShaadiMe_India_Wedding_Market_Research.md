## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

India's wedding industry is a ₹10,79,000 crore (~$130 billion) juggernaut — the country's **fourth-largest industry**, bigger than its entire aviation market. With 80–100 lakh weddings every year, it is the highest-volume wedding market in the world. Average spend has climbed to **₹36.5–39.5 lakh in 2024-25**, and the top segments (₹35L–1Cr+) represent millions of families who are time-poor, stressed, fragmented across dozens of vendors — and almost completely underserved by technology that actually executes rather than just lists.

**ShaadiMe's thesis is sound.** This document lays out exactly how big the target market is, where it lives, what it costs, and how a platform can win it fast.

## SECTION 4 — THE PAIN POINTS (WHY SHAADIМЕ IS NEEDED)

This is the crux of the opportunity. Indian weddings are deeply broken from an execution standpoint. Here's the granular reality:

### 4.1 The Stress Crisis

- **57% of Indian couples find wedding planning extremely stressful** (The Wedding Company survey, 2024)
- **20% find it moderately stressful** — meaning **77% of couples are stressed** during planning
- A 2024 survey by WedMeGood showed couples deal with **8–12 separate vendors** for a single wedding (venue, catering, decor, photography, video, makeup, DJ, transport, invites, mehendi, choreographer, honeymoon)
- **Indian families spend on average twice as much on a single wedding as they do on 18 years of their child's education** (Jefferies 2024)

### 4.2 The Fragmentation Problem

The Indian wedding market is **85–90% unorganised** — dominated by family-run local vendors with:
- No standardised pricing
- No verified reviews or quality control
- No contracts or SLAs
- No online booking or transparency
- Massive variance in actual delivery vs. what was promised

**The result:** "A vendor sends you photos that look like a royal wedding. You approve. You pay. You arrive. And it's giving school annual function." — A reality documented by multiple wedding planners.

### 4.3 The Specific Pain Points (Ranked by Severity)

**Pain Point 1 — Venue Finding (Biggest Bottleneck)**
- 39% of couples cite venue selection as the **most time-consuming and challenging task** (Wedding Company survey 2024)
- Venues book 12–18 months in advance in metros
- Pricing is completely opaque — same venue quotes ₹2–5L variance based on negotiation skills
- Venue inspection means multiple physical visits across the city

**Pain Point 2 — Vendor Quality & Trust**
- No standardized quality verification exists
- Photographers/decorators show "portfolio weddings" that may not represent actual work
- Fake reviews are rampant on freelancer platforms
- Deposit scams and ghosting are common — families lose ₹50,000–5L
- 46% of couples now hire professional wedding planners — but 54% still do it themselves and suffer

**Pain Point 3 — Budget Overrun (Hidden Costs)**
- Families consistently underestimate costs by 20–40%
- "Hidden costs" appear like surprise guests: GST on venues, overtime charges, last-minute décor changes, tip culture, photographer assistant costs
- CAIT data: families often end up spending **3x their annual household income** on weddings
- 12% of couples took loans to fund weddings (WedMeGood 2024)

**Pain Point 4 — Coordination Chaos**
- Average wedding has **10–12 vendors** operating simultaneously across 3–7 days
- There is no single communication or scheduling layer — everything is WhatsApp messages, phone calls, in-person meetings
- Families act as full-time project managers for 6–12 months
- Multiple family stakeholders (parents, in-laws) each separately negotiate with vendors, causing duplication and conflict

**Pain Point 5 — Information Asymmetry**
- Vendors know exactly what the market rate is; families don't
- A couple in Delhi getting married for the first time pays 30–40% more than a couple whose cousin got married 2 years ago
- No platform currently shows real-time, verified pricing for full-package bookings

**Pain Point 6 — Outfit & Jewellery Research**
- 29% of couples cite outfit selection as their second biggest challenge
- Bridal lehenga hunting involves visits to 10–20 stores across 2–3 cities
- No aggregated discovery + price comparison platform for wedding fashion

**Pain Point 7 — Guest Logistics**
- For destination and semi-destination weddings, managing hotel room blocks, airport pickups, transport logistics, and welcome hampers for 100–500 guests is a full-time operation
- NRI couples are especially vulnerable — paying premium rates for poor coordination

### 4.4 The Generation Z & Millennial Shift — The "New Indian Couple"

This shift is critical for ShaadiMe's positioning:

| Old Pattern | New Pattern (ShaadiMe TG) |
|---|---|
| Parents run the entire wedding | Couple is the primary decision-maker |
| Vendor found through family reference | Vendor found through Instagram, Google, apps |
| One family (bride's) pays | 59% equal split, or couple pays directly |
| Offline negotiation only | 40%+ use apps/social to discover vendors |
| Traditional venues preferred | 1 in 4 weddings now destination weddings |
| Gold and diamonds | 46% of brides chose lab-grown diamonds in 2024 |
| Tolerate stress | Demand seamless, managed experiences |
| Paper invitations | Digital invites + WhatsApp RSVP |

**The key behavioral unlock:** Urban millennials aged 26–35 with household incomes of ₹15–50L/year are running their own weddings digitally. They have money, they're pressed for time, and they've already experienced frictionless booking on Swiggy, MakeMyTrip, and Urban Company. They expect the same from wedding planning — but nothing like that exists end-to-end today.

## SECTION 8 — GO-TO-MARKET STRATEGY: BUILD FAST, EARN FAST

### 8.1 Phase 1 — 0 to 90 Days: The Concierge MVP

**Don't build a full platform first. Build a human-assisted booking layer.**

1. **Launch as a WhatsApp-first concierge** — "Tell us your wedding date, city, and budget. We'll send you a complete package quote in 24 hours."
2. **Target: Delhi-NCR + Bangalore** (highest volume + budget + digital adoption)
3. **Curate 30–50 verified vendors per city** across venues, catering, decor, photography
4. **Charge a flat platform/coordination fee** of ₹50,000–1,50,000 per wedding (on ₹35–70L weddings, this is <1% of budget — no-brainer)
5. **First 20 weddings done manually** — your team acts as the ShaadiMe manager
6. **Learnings from these 20 weddings** become the product spec

**Revenue target Month 1–3:** 20 weddings × ₹75,000 = ₹15 lakh
**Revenue target Month 4–6:** 60 weddings × ₹1L = ₹60 lakh

### 8.2 Phase 2 — 90 to 270 Days: Build the Core Product

Based on concierge learnings, build:
1. **Venue discovery + instant quote** (with real pricing, not "call for rates")
2. **Curated vendor bundles** (e.g., "The Udaipur Package" — venue + catering + decor + 2 photographers for ₹48L all-in)
3. **Wedding dashboard** — checklist, vendor tracker, payment milestones, guest list manager
4. **Booking + escrow** — collect 20% upfront, release to vendors at milestones
5. **ShaadiMe Manager** feature — assigned human coordinator for ₹50L+ packages

**Revenue model at scale:**
- Platform/coordination fee: 2–4% of total wedding value
- Vendor subscription (verified badge): ₹5,000–50,000/year per vendor
- Premium curated packages (bundled margin): 8–12% on total package value
- Optional add-ons: Photography album, honeymoon booking, return gift curation

### 8.3 Phase 3 — 9–18 Months: Scale and Defensibility

- Expand to Mumbai, Hyderabad, Pune, Chandigarh
- Launch **Destination Wedding vertical** (₹50L–2Cr) — highest margin, fastest growing
- **NRI weddings** — massive underserved market; NRI couples in the US/UK/Australia planning India weddings. They can't visit vendors. They need exactly what ShaadiMe offers.
- **B2B: Corporate wedding gifts, employee wedding benefits** — hook into HR at large companies
- **Vendor financing** — offer advance payments to vetted vendors in exchange for exclusivity/priority placement

### 8.4 What Gets You to ₹1 Crore Revenue Fast

| Lever | Math |
|---|---|
| 100 weddings at ₹1L platform fee | = ₹1 Crore |
| Or 50 premium packages (₹50L–1Cr weddings) at 3% = ₹1.5–3L fee each | = ₹75L–1.5Cr |
