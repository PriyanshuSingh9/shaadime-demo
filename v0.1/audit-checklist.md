# ShaadiMe Audit Checklist

## Navigation & Header
- **Task**: Brand the header and add nav feedback  
  **Description**: Surface the ShaadiMe wordmark, ensure clear hierarchy, and give visitors section context via nav states.  
  **Subtasks**:
  - Render the ShaadiMe logo/wordmark consistently at the left edge of the navigation on all breakpoints and network speeds.  
  - Add active-state styling (bolding, underline, or color shift) to nav links that updates as the user scrolls.  
  - Align the CTA copy (“Start Planning →” vs “Start my wedding planning →”) so both placements share a single, intentional message.

## Hero Section
- **Task**: Refine hero copy and CTA focus  
  **Description**: Smooth the hero headline rhythm, reinforce the emotional story, and simplify the CTA structure to a single, decisive action.  
  **Subtasks**:
  - Adjust line breaks so “Craft a wedding worth / arriving for” reads naturally instead of splitting “worth arriving for”.  
  - Trim the subtext and replace “stay present for the moments that matter” with a more unique emotional promise.  
  - Replace the dual CTA (primary + secondary) with a single conversion action; make “Explore themes” a secondary anchor link.  
  - Tweak the stat row so “Full planning support” reads like a descriptive label tied to a real numeric stat.  
  - Verify hero videos feature authentic South Indian wedding imagery and swap any generic footage.

## Themes, Venues & Decor
- **Task**: Resolve image reuse and deepen descriptions  
  **Description**: Stop repeating stock photos across sections and provide the same rich storytelling in the decor cards that exists for themes.  
  **Subtasks**:
  - Replace duplicated images (e.g., pexels-photo-33361433 used across Theme/Venue/Decor) with curated assets unique to each section.  
  - Update the “Traditional South Indian” theme image to reflect genuine cultural cues (Kanjivaram, mandap, jasmine, etc.).  
  - Add Fusion or clarify absence and expand decor cards with evocative one-line descriptions, matching the narrative level used in themes.  
  - Add a short subtext for the Venue section to match the tone set in Themes.

## Why ShaadiMe
- **Task**: Elevate the value pillar block  
  **Description**: Align the iconography and imagery with the premium brand voice and ensure numbering doesn’t compete with the headings.  
  **Subtasks**:
  - Replace emoji icons with bespoke or premium vector icons that match the overall palette.  
  - Reduce the visual weight of the section numbers so they stay secondary (lighter color, smaller size/weight).  
  - Swap the right-hand collage for South Indian wedding photography that supports each value pillar claim.

## Intake Flow
- **Task**: Build the PRD-specified multi-step form  
  **Description**: Deliver the 9-step intake flow, capture critical data up front, and make the inline CTA a gateway to the form.  
  **Subtasks**:
  - Implement the multi-screen form (one question per screen, progress indicator, step micro-copy, transitions) from PRD Section 8.  
  - Collect couple names and phone number in Steps 1 and 2, followed by wedding date, budget, wedding type, guest count, planning preference, inspiration, and city.  
  - Remove the checkbox-based service selector from the homepage; let the form recommend services after intake.  
  - Create the confirmation screen described in PRD Section 8.3 with animation and personalized messaging.  
  - Keep the inline CTA as a “Start the form” trigger that launches the multi-step experience.

## Launch Cities
- **Task**: Match imagery to the promise  
  **Description**: Serve consistent, on-brand photography and avoid mixing CDN vs local images.  
  **Subtasks**:
  - Host the Hyderabad photo via the CDN (or replace it with an optimized local asset) and ensure parity with Bengaluru and Chennai images.  
  - Replace skyline shots with editorial wedding imagery that shows what a ShaadiMe celebration looks like in each city.

## FAQ & Footer
- **Task**: Close trust gaps in copy and navigation  
  **Description**: Add clarity on cost and next steps, and make the footer a confident brand statement with functioning links.  
  **Subtasks**:
  - Include a ballpark range in the “How much does it cost” FAQ answer to address researched couples.  
  - Add a FAQ entry that explains what happens after form submission (call timing, who calls, what to expect).  
  - Point the Privacy and Contact footer links to real pages, add active Instagram/social links, and craft a closing tagline that reinforces the brand promise.

## Priority Tracking
- **Task**: Categorize fixes per audit severity  
  **Description**: Translate the final priority tiers into actionable tracking for the team.  
  **Subtasks**:
  - Label image reuse, CTA inconsistency, and intake form/data capture as P0 (launch blocker) and ensure they are addressed before public sharing.  
  - Mark CTA copy, hero rhythm fixes, iconography swaps, and inline form flow as P1 to ship before launch.  
  - Track remaining UX polish items (e.g., decor prose, venue subtext, FAQ additions) as P2 for post-launch scale.
